# Research & Analysis — Prompt Templates

Use these by replacing [BRACKETS] with your specifics and pasting into ChatGPT.

## 1) Market Landscape Summary
ROLE: Market research expert  
TONE: Objective, concise  
TASK: For [INDUSTRY/NICHE], produce a 5‑section market landscape: (1) market size & growth, (2) customer segments, (3) key players, (4) opportunities, (5) risks & barriers. Use frameworks (Porter/PESTLE/SWOT) as appropriate and note sources if asked. Keep each section ≤100 words.  
OUTPUT FORMAT: Labeled sections.  
CONSTRAINTS: Avoid speculation without stated assumptions.

## 2) Competitor Deep‑Dive
ROLE: Competitive intelligence analyst  
TONE: Analytical, tactical  
TASK: For competitors [LIST] or "top public competitors in [INDUSTRY]", produce 5 short profiles: overview, strengths, weaknesses, pricing/monetization, recent moves (product/partnerships). Finish with 3 gap/opportunity ideas and one recommendation to differentiate. Each profile ≤100 words.  
OUTPUT FORMAT: Competitor profiles + Gaps + Recommendation.

## 3) Technology Feasibility Assessment
ROLE: Technology assessment expert  
TONE: Practical, evidence-focused  
TASK: For technology concept [DESCRIBE], assess: TRL or readiness, key technical risks, required resources, time-to-minimum-viable-prototype, and one go/no-go criterion. Provide 3 suggested low-cost validation experiments. Keep sections concise.  
OUTPUT FORMAT: Sections (Readiness, Risks, Resources, Timeline, Experiments).

## 4) Customer Discovery Question Set
ROLE: Customer research lead  
TONE: Curious, non-leading  
TASK: For target user [INSERT AUDIENCE], generate 10–15 open-ended interview questions designed to surface pain points, workflows, and willingness to pay. Keep each question <25 words and include 1–2 follow-up probes per key question.  
OUTPUT FORMAT: Numbered list of questions with optional probes.

## 5) Quick Research Checklist (use after running a prompt)
- Verify at least one external source or data point for any major claim.  
- Flag assumptions you want the model to check.  
- Ask for a 2‑sentence summary for an executive.
